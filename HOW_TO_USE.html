How To Use AI Trade Terminal

This guide explains everything — how signals are created, how to read them, how to trade manually or automatically, and how the safety system protects your account.

🧠 What Is This System?

This is an AI-powered crypto trading assistant. It watches the market 24/7, analyzes price patterns using professional trading methods, and tells you — or acts automatically — when a good trade setup appears.

📊 Market Analysis

Fetches real price data from Binance every few seconds and runs technical indicators

🤖 Claude AI Brain

Anthropic's Claude AI reads all the data and decides if there's a valid trade setup

🛡️ Risk Guard

Automatic position sizing, stop losses, and daily loss limits protect your account

Think of it like this
The system works like having a professional trader watching charts 24/7. It spots setups, calculates the risk, tells you exactly where to enter, where to put your stop loss, and where to take profit — then either tells you or executes it for you.

🚀 How To Start

1

Open a terminal in your project folder and run: python main.py
You should see: Listening: http://127.0.0.1:8000

Double-click trading_dashboard.html in your file browser. The status pills in the top-right corner should turn green within a few seconds.

4

Pick a market and click ANALYZE

Select BTC/USDT (or any pair) from the left panel, then click the blue 🤖 ANALYZE button. Claude will analyze the market and return a signal in 5–15 seconds.

⚙️ How Signals Are Made

Every signal goes through 6 layers of analysis before Claude makes a decision:

📥
Step 1 — Fetch Live Market Data The system downloads the last 250 hourly candles (price bars) for your chosen pair from Binance. This gives Claude real, accurate price history to work with.
↓
📐
Step 2 — Calculate All Indicators It automatically computes: EMA 20/50/200 (trend direction), RSI (overbought/oversold), MACD (momentum), ATR (volatility), Bollinger Bands (price range), and Volume SMA (is volume normal or spiking?).
↓
⏱️
Step 3 — Multi-Timeframe Check (15m / 1h / 4h) The system checks if the market is trending up or down on three different timeframes. If at least 2 out of 3 agree (e.g. 1h and 4h both look bullish), the signal gets stronger. If they disagree, the system says WAIT.
↓
😨
Step 4 — Fear & Greed Index The system fetches the live Crypto Fear & Greed Index (0–100). Extreme Fear <25 = markets are panicking, look for LONG opportunities. Extreme Greed >75 = markets are overconfident, be cautious on longs.
↓
🏦
Step 5 — Smart Money Concepts (SMC) This is institutional trading logic. The system finds: Order Blocks (where big banks placed orders), Fair Value Gaps (price gaps institutions want to fill), and Liquidity Levels (where stop losses are clustered — banks hunt these). These are the exact zones where high-probability trades happen.
↓
🤖
Step 6 — Claude AI Makes The Decision All the data above is sent to Claude AI in one detailed prompt. Claude analyzes everything together — like an expert trader reading the full picture — and returns a structured JSON signal with action, prices, and reasoning.

🖱️ Manual Trading (Signals Only)

In Manual Mode (AUTO_TRADE=false), the system gives you signals but you decide whether to act on them.

1

Get a signal

Click 🤖 ANALYZE on a pair, or click ⚡ SCAN ALL to scan all 4 pairs at once. Wait for the signal to appear in the bottom panel.

2

Check the action and confidence

Only consider acting if: Action = LONG or SHORT AND confidence ≥ 68%. If it says WAIT, do nothing.

3

Read the entry, SL, and TPs

Note the Entry price, SL (Stop Loss), and three TP levels. These are calculated using ATR (market volatility), so they are realistic for current conditions.

4

Go to WEEX and place the trade

Open your WEEX app. Set a Limit Order at the entry price. Place your stop loss order immediately. Set TP1 as your first take profit target.

5

Manage the trade

When TP1 hits → move your stop loss to breakeven (entry price). When TP2 hits → move stop loss to TP1 (you're now guaranteed profit). Let the remaining 20% run to TP3.

⚠ Golden Rule
Never move your stop loss further away from your entry to "give the trade more room." Only move it to lock in profits (toward TP). A stop loss exists to protect your capital — never widen it.

📍 Entry, Stop Loss & Take Profit Explained

📥 Entry Price

The price you open your position at. The system uses a limit order slightly below current price for LONG (or above for SHORT) to get a better fill than market price.

🛑 Stop Loss (SL)

If price moves against you and hits this level, the trade closes automatically. This is set using 1.5× ATR from entry — wide enough to avoid normal market noise, tight enough to limit loss.

🎯 TP1 — 50% of position

First profit target. When hit, close half the position and move SL to breakeven. This locks in safety — you can't lose money even if the rest goes wrong.

🎯 TP2 — 30% of position

Second profit target. When hit, close 30% more and move SL to TP1. You're now guaranteed profit on the whole trade no matter what.

🚀 TP3 — 20% runner

Leave the last 20% running with a trailing stop. This is your "lottery ticket" — if the market makes a big move, this portion captures the full run. If not, your trailing stop protects profits from TP1 and TP2.

⚠️ Safety Rules — Never Break These

🔴 CRITICAL — Read Before Going Live

These rules are hardcoded into the system. Never override them manually.

!

Stop Loss is MANDATORY

A stop loss order is placed immediately after every entry. The system will never hold a position without a stop loss. No exceptions.

!

Never risk more than MAX_TRADE_USDT

Currently set to $150 in your .env. With a $110 account this should be reduced to $10–$15. Update it to match your balance.

!

Every action is logged

Every signal, attempt, success, and failure is written to trades.log. Review this file regularly to understand what the bot is doing.

!

WEEX API errors never crash the bot

If any order fails on WEEX, the error is logged and skipped — the bot continues running safely. It never retries an order blindly.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Why does Claude say WAIT most of the time?

This is correct and expected. A professional trader only takes 2–5 high-quality setups per day, not every candle. WAIT means "the market conditions don't give a clear edge right now." Forcing trades in a WAIT market is how accounts get blown.

What does "Mixed TF Bias" mean?

It means the 15m, 1h, and 4h timeframes don't all agree on direction. For example, 15m says bearish, 1h says bullish, 4h says neutral. Without agreement, there's no clean trade — this is a WAIT scenario.

Can I change the pairs it scans?

Yes. Edit the SYMBOLS line in your .env file: SYMBOLS=BTC/USDT,ETH/USDT,SOL/USDT and restart the server.

How do I know if a trade was actually placed on WEEX?

Check trades.log in your project folder — every action is logged. You can also go to WEEX app → Orders → Open Orders to see live orders.

The bot paused itself — what happened?

Either 3 consecutive losses occurred (2-hour pause) or a single loss exceeded 3% of your account (1-hour pause). Check the STATS tab in the dashboard — it shows the pause reason and how many minutes remain.

What is Fear & Greed and why does it matter?

It's a daily market sentiment score (0=extreme fear, 100=extreme greed). When markets are in extreme fear, retail traders are panic selling — which is often when smart money buys. When markets are extremely greedy, everyone is overleveraged — which is when corrections happen. The system uses this as a contrarian signal.

Is this a guaranteed profit system?

No. No trading system guarantees profits. This system uses professional analysis methods to find high-probability setups, but all trades carry risk. The risk management system is designed to ensure losses stay small and manageable. Always trade with money you can afford to lose.
